The problem RailIQ solves

Rail operators monitor infrastructure risks and equipment performance, but critical insights often live in separate products, service teams, and workflows. RailIQ brings these signals together so railroads can move from fragmented monitoring to coordinated action.

What RailIQ combines

RailIQ reframes L.B. Foster’s Total Track Monitoring and Anatomy Asset Intelligence into one modern cloud platform that is easy for operations, maintenance, and engineering teams to understand.

Unified portfolio

Total Track Monitoring

Pulls in field intelligence from rockfall, flood, and wheel impact monitoring so users see site status, risk signals, and escalation logic in one view.

  • Rockfall detection and obstruction awareness
  • Flood thresholds with remote confirmation
  • Wheel impact trend monitoring and exception review

Anatomy Asset Intelligence

Adds asset condition context for gauge-face and wheel flange lubrication, trackside solutions, and onboard systems.

  • Asset uptime and maintenance visibility
  • Consumable and refill forecasting
  • Service history and route planning support

Predictive cloud layer

Normalizes data, ranks service urgency, enables remote diagnostics, and recommends proactive field scheduling with realistic next steps.

  • Cross-domain AI scoring
  • Maintenance recommendation engine
  • Action queue for service teams

How RailIQ operates

The platform follows a simple sequence from data collection to field work, making it clear how monitoring intelligence becomes maintenance action.

01
Collect

Bring track monitoring alerts, asset condition data, and consumable performance into a shared data layer.

02
Analyze

Score risk across environmental exposure, wheel impacts, uptime, and lubrication confidence.

03
Diagnose

Use remote diagnostics first so teams verify issues before dispatching field crews.

04
Schedule

Bundle proactive service work by corridor and urgency to reduce truck rolls and unplanned work.
