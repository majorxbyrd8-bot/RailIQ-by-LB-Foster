The challenge rail operators face

Rail networks generate alarms from many different systems, but those alerts often sit in separate tools and teams. Control rooms, maintenance planners, and engineering groups see parts of the picture, yet it is hard to agree on priorities or schedule work efficiently.

How RailIQ brings data together

RailIQ connects safety monitoring, asset condition data, and maintenance information into one operating view. It is designed so control rooms and field teams can work from the same set of facts.

Single view

Network monitoring

Ingests events from existing monitoring systems and maps them to locations, lines, and routes across your network.

  • Site‑level status for key locations
  • Context on weather and operating conditions
  • History of recent events for each asset

Asset health

Tracks the status and service needs of critical wayside and onboard equipment, including friction‑management assets.

  • Uptime and fault history
  • Refill and service forecasts
  • Service history by location and route

Decision engine

Applies rules and analytics to score risk, group related events, and generate clear recommendations for next actions.

  • Risk scoring for each event
  • Automatic grouping by corridor
  • Suggested actions for operations teams

How RailIQ fits into daily operations

The platform follows a simple sequence from data collection to field work, so teams can move from alarms to coordinated action quickly.

01
Collect

RailIQ continuously ingests alerts and asset data from connected systems.

02
Prioritize

Events are scored by risk and impact, then grouped by corridor or site.

03
Diagnose

Operations teams review high‑priority items first and use remote checks where possible.

04
Dispatch & plan

Confirmed issues become targeted work orders, helping planners bundle jobs and reduce urgent call‑outs.

Example workflow

AlertRepeated high‑impact readings at a site

ActionBundled route for field crews

Maintenance visits are combined with nearby inspections to maximize each trip.
